How it works: The AD curve slopes downward. The SRAS is horizontal (sticky prices). The LRAS is vertical at potential output. Monetary policy shifts AD left or right. Cost shocks shift SRAS up or down.

Try these scenarios:

  • Expansionary policy: Increase M → AD shifts right → P and Y rise
  • Negative supply shock: Increase costs → SRAS shifts up → P rises, Y falls (stagflation)
  • Positive supply shock: Decrease costs → SRAS shifts down → P falls, Y rises
